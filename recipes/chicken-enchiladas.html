Chicken Enchiladas

Description

Chicken enchiladas with sour cream sauce are one of my mothers favorite dishes so it had to be added to the list of recipes. Chicken enchiladas are a staple of Tex-Mex here in Texas.

Earnesto's in Frisco, Texas is a great place to get quality chicken enchiladas.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of Chicken Breast
  • 1 Medium Onion, Minced
  • 1 Tablespoon of Vegetable Oil
  • 8 - 8 inch Flour Tortillas, Softened
  • 1.5 Cups of Grated Monterey Jack Cheese or 1.5 Cups Mexican Blend Cheese, Divided
  • 1/4 Cup of Butter
  • 1/4 Cup of Flour
  • 1 - 15 oz Can of Chicken Broth
  • 1 Cup of Sour Cream
  • 1 - 4 oz Can Chopped Green Chillies or 2 Fresh Chili Peppers, Minced

Steps

  1. In a frying pan, cook chicken and onion together in oil over medium-high heat until chicken is just done.
  2. Divide cooked chicken evenly between 8 tortillas; add 1 1/2 tablespoons cheese to each tortilla.
  3. Roll enchiladas and place seam-side down in a 9x13" baking dish that has been lightly sprayed with no-stick cooking spray.
  4. Melt butter in a medium saucepan; stir in flour to make a roux, stir and cook until bubbly, and gradually whisk in chicken broth then bring to boiling, stirring frequently.
  5. Remove from heat; stir in sour cream and green chilies.
  6. Pour sauce evenly over enchiladas.
  7. Top with remaining 3/4 cup cheese (a baking dish may be double-wrapped and frozen at this point) and bake at 400Â° F for 20 minutes until cheese is melted and sauce near edges of baking dish is bubbly.
